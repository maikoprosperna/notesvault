4 C's of a Diamond

When designing your dream ring, you’ll be able to select for carat, color, clarity, and cut. Read below to get to know the process for designing your perfect ring.

  • Carat Weight
  • Color
  • Clarity
  • Cut

Carat Weight

Diamond carat weight is the measurement of how much a diamond weights. Each carat can be subdivided into 100 points.Many people assume that prices increase the more carats a diamond weighs, but two diamonds of equal carat weight can be priced very differently dependin g on their clarity, color, and cut.

Color

Diamond color refers to how colorless a diamond is. The less color, the higher the grade. The Gemological Institute of America (GIA) grades diamond color on a scale from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown). Even a slight difference in color can affect a diamond’s value and visual appeal.

Clarity

Clarity measures the presence of internal or external imperfections, called inclusions and blemishes. Most are microscopic and do not affect a diamond’s beauty, but the fewer the imperfections, the higher the clarity grade and price. Common grades range from Flawless (FL) to Included (I).

Cut

Cut is arguably the most important factor affecting a diamond's sparkle and brilliance. It refers to how well a diamond’s facets interact with light. A well-cut diamond will appear more radiant and lively. Grades range from Excellent to Poor, and even a perfectly colorless diamond can appear dull if poorly cut.
